COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS

Children don't experience mental health problems.

Young children may show early warning signs of mental health concerns. They can be clinically diagnosed and treated.

People with mental health problems are violent and unpredictable.

People can be highly active and productive members of a community while having a mental illness.

People with mental illnesses never get better.

Mental illnesses are treatable, and with the right kind of help, most people do recover and lead healthy, productive, and satisfying lives.

People with mental illnesses can "pull themselves out of it".

A mental illness is not caused by personal weakness, and is not cured by personal strength.

Treatment is a waste of time

Whether it is medication or therapy, treatment can be extremely beneficial.

Mental illness isn't common

At least 18% of the total adult population in the U.S. suffer from some mental illness.

I can't do anything for someone with mental illnesses.

You can do many things to help people with mental illnesses, such as treating them with the respect and dignity that they deserve.

People are faking or doing it for attention.

No one would choose to have a mental illness, just as no one would choose to have a physical illness.

Mental illnesses will never effect me

Family, friends, and co-workers can have a mental illness.

Everyone gets depressed as they grow older. It's just a part of the aging process

Older adults have a higher risk of depression, but it's not an inevitable part of aging.
